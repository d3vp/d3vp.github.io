Reduced row-echelon form

A matrix is in reduced row-echelon form (RREF) if it satisfies all of the following conditions.

  • In every row the leftmost non-zero entry is 111 (called the leading 111).

  • If a column contains a leading 111 (i.e. first non-zero entry in the column is 111), then all other entries in that column is 000.

  • The leading 111 of any given row is always to the right of the leading 111 of the row above it.

  • Any rows consisting entirely of zeroes are placed at the bottom of the matrix.

Elementary row operations

We use the following elementary operations to convert a matrix M into RREF:

  1. Row Swap. Exchange any two rows: M[i]↔M[j]M[i] \leftrightarrow M[j]M[i]↔M[j]

  2. Scalar Multiplication. Multiply any row by a non-zero constant:
    M[i]←k⋅M[i],k≠0M[i] \gets k \cdot M[i] , k \neq 0M[i]←k⋅M[i],k=0

  3. Row Addition. Add multiple of one row to another row:
    M[i]←M[i]+k⋅M[j],i≠jM[i] \gets M[i] + k \cdot M[j] , i \neq jM[i]←M[i]+k⋅M[j],i=j
